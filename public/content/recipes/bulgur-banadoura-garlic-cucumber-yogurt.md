## Ingredients

### Bulgur Pilav

- 1 kg tomatoes, peeled and diced  
- 320 g coarse bulgur  
- 1 large onion, finely diced  
- 2 cloves garlic, crushed  
- 1 medium bell pepper (capsicum), finely diced  
- 1 small green chili, finely diced  
- ¼ tsp paprika (ground)  
- ¼ tsp cumin (ground)  
- 1 can chickpeas, drained  
- 1 ½ tbsp tomato paste  
- ½ cup extra virgin olive oil  
- 1 ¾ cups water  
- 1 tbsp salt  

### Garlic-Cucumber Yogurt

- 1 ½ cups plain yogurt  
- ½ tsp crushed garlic  
- ¼ cup finely diced cucumber  
- ½ tsp dried mint  

## Instructions

### Bulgur Pilav

1. Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium-high heat.  
2. Add the onion and garlic and sauté until translucent.  
3. Stir in the tomato paste, then add bell pepper, green chili, tomatoes, paprika, cumin, and salt. Mix well.  
4. Pour in the water and bring to a simmer.  
5. Add the bulgur and chickpeas, stirring to combine.  
6. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for about 25 minutes.  
7. Turn off the heat and let the pot rest, covered, for 10 minutes to absorb the remaining liquid.  
8. Fluff the bulgur gently with a fork.  
9. Garnish with fresh parsley and sliced red onion, if desired.  

### Garlic-Cucumber Yogurt

1. In a bowl, combine the yogurt, garlic, cucumber, and dried mint.  
2. Mix well and chill until ready to serve.  

### To Serve

1. Spoon the warm bulgur pilav onto plates.  
2. Serve with a generous dollop of garlic-cucumber yogurt on the side.
