## Ingredients

### Cake
- 250 g unsalted butter, chopped  
- 200 g 70% dark chocolate, finely chopped  
- 1½ cups (330 ml) milk  
- 1 cup (220 g) caster sugar  
- 1 cup (175 g) light brown sugar  
- 1 tsp vanilla extract  
- 2 eggs  
- 1¼ cups (260 g) self-raising flour (or flour + 3 tsp baking powder)  
- ¾ cup (95 g) cocoa powder, sifted

### Glaze
- 200 g 70% dark chocolate, finely chopped  
- 100 g unsalted butter, chopped  
- 1/4 cup (90 g) liquid glucose  
- 1 tsp vegetable oil

## Method
1. Preheat oven to 160°C (325°F). Grease a 2.75-litre Bundt tin.  
2. In a medium saucepan, melt the butter and chocolate over low heat, stirring until smooth and combined.  
3. In a large bowl, combine milk, caster sugar, light brown sugar, and vanilla; whisk until smooth.  
4. Add the melted chocolate mixture and eggs; whisk until fully combined.  
5. Sift in the flour and cocoa; whisk until the batter is smooth.  
6. Pour into the prepared tin and bake for 35–45 minutes, or until a skewer comes out clean.  
7. Cool in the tin for 30 minutes, then invert onto a rack.  
8. For the glaze, melt chocolate, butter, and glucose in a saucepan over low heat, stirring until smooth. Stir in oil.  
9. Place the cooled cake on a rack set over a tray and pour the glaze over the top.
