## Ingredients

### For the Dough

- 1.5 kg bread flour
- 1 1/2 tbsp instant yeast
- 1 1/3 to 1 3/4 cups water, or more if needed
- 1/4 tbsp salt

### For the Filling

- 1 kg fresh zaatar leaves
- 1 1/2 large onions, finely chopped
- 2 tbsp sumac
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- Salt, to taste
- Black pepper, to taste

### For Baking

- Around 10 tbsp extra virgin olive oil

## Instructions

### Prepare the Dough

1. Mix the flour, salt, and yeast. Gradually add as much water as needed until you get a soft, slightly sticky dough.
2. Knead until smooth, place in an oiled bowl, cover, and let rise for 1 hour.

### Prepare the Filling

1. Wash the zaatar well and squeeze out all excess water.
2. Sprinkle with salt and rub the leaves well with your hands.
3. In a separate bowl, mix the onions with the sumac, black pepper, and olive oil.
4. Add the onion mixture to the zaatar leaves and mix everything together.

### Shape and Bake

1. Divide the dough into 4 portions and flatten each one into a thin round sheet.
2. Spread a thin layer of zaatar filling on top.
3. Fold the right edge of the dough toward the center to cover the filling, add another thin layer of zaatar, then fold the left edge over everything.
4. Flatten slightly and spread one more layer of zaatar filling.
5. Fold the top edge all the way down to the bottom edge. You should end up with a square-shaped dough filled with zaatar.
6. Let it rest for 10 minutes.
7. Preheat the oven to 180°C.
8. Heat a little olive oil in the tray, place the dough on top, and gently stretch it out.
9. Bake for about 10 minutes or until golden and crispy underneath. Flip for extra crispiness if desired.
10. Serve warm with tea, laban labneh, cheese, or tomato slices on the side.
