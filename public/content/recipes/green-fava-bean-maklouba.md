## Ingredients

- 1 kg green fava beans
- 2 onions, chopped
- Olive oil
- 300 g minced lamb
- 1 tsp black pepper
- 1 tsp cinnamon
- 1 tsp cumin
- 1 tsp seven spices
- Black kale leaves
- 2 cups basmati rice, washed and drained
- 2 tsp turmeric
- 5 cups meat or vegetable stock
- Toasted almonds and pine nuts, for garnish
- Lebanese yogurt, for serving

## Instructions

1. Remove the tough stems from the fava beans. If the outer shell is tender, keep it on; if it is tough, peel it off.
2. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil and cook the fava beans until tender. Drain and set aside.
3. In a large pot, heat a generous drizzle of olive oil and cook the chopped onions until soft and translucent.
4. Add the minced lamb and cook until browned. Season with black pepper, cinnamon, cumin, and seven spices. Stir well to combine.
5. Layer the cooked fava beans over the meat mixture. Arrange black kale leaves around the edges of the pot to help hold the maklouba together when flipped.
6. Mix the drained basmati rice with the turmeric, then spread it evenly over the fava beans.
7. Place a plate over the rice to keep the layers intact. Carefully pour in the stock.
8. Cover the pot and cook over medium heat until the liquid starts bubbling. Reduce the heat to low and cook for another 20 to 25 minutes, or until the rice is fully cooked.
9. Remove from the heat and let the maklouba rest for 10 minutes.
10. Flip onto a serving platter and garnish with toasted almonds and pine nuts.
11. Serve warm with Lebanese yogurt.
