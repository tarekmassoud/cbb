## Ingredients

### For the Kishk Soup

- 500 g lamb chunks (shoulder or leg, cut into small cubes)
- 1 whole head garlic, finely diced
- 1 1/2 cups dry kishk powder
- 5 cups water, plus extra if needed
- 1 tbsp olive oil, plus more for serving

### Traditional Sides

- Fresh radishes
- 1 onion, cut into wedges
- Fresh mint leaves
- Lebanese bread

## Instructions

### Kishk Soup

1. Heat the olive oil in a pot over medium heat.
2. Add the lamb chunks and sear until lightly browned.
3. Add the garlic and sauté for 1 minute until fragrant.
4. Pour in the water, cover, and let the lamb simmer until tender, about 35 to 45 minutes. You should have tender lamb with a small amount of flavorful broth left in the pot.
5. Stir in the kishk powder.
6. Cook over low heat, stirring constantly to prevent sticking.
7. Simmer until the soup thickens to a creamy consistency, about 10 to 15 minutes.
8. If it becomes too thick, add a little hot water to loosen it.
9. Serve hot with a drizzle of olive oil and the fresh sides.
