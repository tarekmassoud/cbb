## Ingredients

- 4–5 salmon fillets  
- 1 tbsp extra-virgin olive oil  
- 3 garlic cloves, thinly sliced  
- 1 tbsp all-purpose flour  
- 1 cup dry white wine  
- 2 tbsp capers, rinsed  
- Juice of 1 lemon  
- Lemon segments, supremed  
- 3 tbsp fresh parsley, chopped  
- Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper  
- 3 tbsp cold butter, cubed  

## Instructions

1. Prepare the lemon segments, reserving the juice.
2. Season the salmon generously with salt and pepper.
3. Heat olive oil in a stainless-steel pan over medium-high heat.
4. Sear the salmon skin-side down until nicely browned.
5. Transfer the salmon to a lightly greased baking tray and bake at 190°C for 10 minutes.
6. In the same pan, melt 1 tbsp butter and sauté the garlic gently.
7. Stir in the flour and cook briefly.
8. Deglaze with white wine and lemon juice, stirring constantly.
9. Reduce to a simmer until slightly thickened.
10. Add capers and lemon segments, season to taste.
11. Whisk in the remaining cold butter until glossy.
12. Finish with parsley and serve over the salmon.
